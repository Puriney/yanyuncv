Data scientist

The Pickrell Lab at the New York Genome Center is looking for a data scientist to contribute to the group’s work on applying large-scale genomic data to fundamental biological problems. The successful applicant will:

✔   Generally speaking, Yun would highly match the position exploring fundamental mechanism with NGS analysis approaches, because of his intrinsic molecular biology background together with bioinformatics skills.

    Set up and improve upon tools for visualization of high-throughput sequencing data;

    ✔   See his previous small demo, Reproduce ENCODE/CSHL Long RNA-seq data visualization viewed in UCSC, posted approximately 15 months ago on biostars forum http://www.biostars.org/p/54351/. Very recently he began enriching his public "exhibition" of plots/figures for NGS data analysis (http://puriney.tumblr.com/), of which the R language scripts were deployed at GitHub (https://github.com/Puriney/Rtist).

    Implement web applications to distribute and interact with genomic data;

    ✓   Admittedly Yun had no experience in SQL, he was active Galaxy platform user, as well as UCSC genome browser, where sharing and interacting with data took place. Presenting the biological meanings underlying data was and is his priority.

    Develop novel methods for analysis of RNA-seq, ChIP-seq, and related technologies

Although processing and understanding data is less submissive to creating wheel at his current career stage, more details about the less standardized repository could be temporarily available at http://puriney.github.io/CLIPseqToolkit/.
